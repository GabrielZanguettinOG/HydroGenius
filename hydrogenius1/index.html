AI for Ocean Worlds

Predicting Ecosystem Evolution on Distant Worlds

Our project envisions an AI capable of predicting the future ecosystem, atmosphere, and surface conditions of ocean worlds based on key chemical characteristics such as molecular composition, energy sources like chemosynthesis and hydrothermal activity, average temperature, and atmospheric factors. The AI simulates how life could thrive in such extreme environments over millions of years, expanding our understanding of habitability beyond Earth.

Using data from real celestial bodies like Enceladus and Titan, the AI builds predictions of how potential life forms may evolve and adapt in these extreme settings.

Our AI utilizes real-world data to forecast the development of ecosystems on ocean worlds. By analyzing various environmental factors, including chemical compositions, energy sources, and atmospheric conditions, the AI generates accurate predictions about how these unique ecosystems may evolve over time. This approach not only enhances our understanding of potential life forms but also aids in identifying the most promising celestial bodies for future exploration.
